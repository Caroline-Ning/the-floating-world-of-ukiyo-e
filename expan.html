Wrapping Paper

It is said that ukiyo-e was disseminated outside Japan during the national isolation period during the Edo era. Ukiyo-e prints first appeared in Europe as packaging material used to protect valuable imported porcelain objects. Later, this “wrapping paper” became popular, and some traders purchased ukiyo-e woodblock prints as art.

Japonisme movement

By being exhibited at the World Exposition held in Paris, France, in 1867, ukiyo-e brought a significant art movement based on traditional Japanese style to Europe.
Some art scholars believe that Ukiyo-e woodblock prints had a huge influence on the Impressionism, Post-Impressionism & the Nouveau art movements. This Japanese art form introduced the concepts of flat planes of color, asymmetrical compositions, unconventional poses, and everyday scenes into art, which inspired the European artist in content, style, and approach, and led to the Japonisme movement in art and design.

An international dealer

Tadamasa Hayashi (林忠正, 1853–1906) was a Japanese art dealer who introduced traditional Japanese art such as ukiyo-e to Europe. He made friends with Impressionist painters who were creating new paintings inspired by ukiyo-e and introduced Impressionist works to Japan for the first time. He is said to be the only Japanese close to Edouard Manet.
