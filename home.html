Living only for the moment, savoring the moon, the snow, the cherry blossoms, and the maple leaves, singing songs, drinking wine, and diverting oneself in simply floating, unconcerned by the prospect of imminent poverty, buoyant and carefree, like a gourd carried along with the current of the river. This what we call the "floating world."

——Asai Ryoi, Tales of the Floating World

Some art scholars believe that Ukiyo-e woodblock prints had a huge influence on the Impressionism, Post-Impressionism & the Nouveau art movements. Ukiyo-e inspired European artists such as Oscar-Claude Monet, Vincent Van Gogh, and Henri de Toulouse-Lautrec and led to the Japonisme movement in art and design.
